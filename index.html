Scher’s Career

She then completed a move to New York in the 70s to begin her professional career where she worked as a layout artist at random House’s Children’s book division. She decided to leave this job and found another one in CBS records in the advertisement and promotion department. Two years later she joined a competing label, Atlantic Records, as an art director. This is where she

designed her first album cover. This led to her later returning to CBS records for eight years producing over 150 album covers annually. After eight years, in 1982, she resigned from CBS records. Her typography was influenced by Russian constructivism and without imitating its style she only made use of its vocabulary form.

Own Brand

In 1984, she decided to team up with a fellow graduate, Tyler Koppel, an editorial designer. They created a firm named Kopper and Scher. They ran it for seven years where she designed the iconic Swatch poster after the Swiss designer Herbert Matters work. Then in 1991, Koppel left the company to become a creative director for Esquire magazine. Scher also decided to leave as the company was crumbling after the recession. She left for Pentagram where she became phenomenally successful, she began in the design studio as partner of the company and eventually worked her way up the ladder to become the principal.

Teaching

While working for Pentagram, she began teaching at the School of Visual Arts and occasionally at prestigious art institutions including Yale and Tyler school of Art and Cooper Union.
