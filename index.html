Scher’s Career

She then completed a move to New York in the 70s to begin her professional career where she worked as a layout artist at random House’s Children’s book division. She decided to leave this job and found another one in CBS records in the advertisement and promotion department. Two years later she joined a competing label, Atlantic Records, as an art director. This is where she

designed her first album cover. This led to her later returning to CBS records for eight years producing over 150 album covers annually. After eight years, in 1982, she resigned from CBS records. Her typography was influenced by Russian constructivism and without imitating its style she only made use of its vocabulary form.

Own Brand

In 1984, she decided to team up with a fellow graduate, Tyler Koppel, an editorial designer. They created a firm named Kopper and Scher. They ran it for seven years where she designed the iconic Swatch poster after the Swiss designer Herbert Matters work. Then in 1991, Koppel left the company to become a creative director for Esquire magazine. Scher also decided to leave as the company was crumbling after the recession. She left for Pentagram where she became phenomenally successful, she began in the design studio as partner of the company and eventually worked her way up the ladder to become the principal.

Teaching

While working for Pentagram, she began teaching at the School of Visual Arts and occasionally at prestigious art institutions including Yale and Tyler school of Art and Cooper Union.

Her Private Life

Scher married her husband Seymour Chwast' in 1973, but just five years after the marriage the couple divorced. In 1989 they remarried and have been together ever since. He was a huge influence on her work before they met as he created work with a clever blending of imagery and type.

Due to the success of the exhibition, the gallery owner extended the show for an additional four weeks, and to accommodate the extension of the exhibition, they produced silk-screened map prints of cities, states and continents which were entitled The following year, she created screen-prints of the New York City transit routes. She has also produced a limited-edition collection of maps of China that represent the rapid growth of the industry of the country as an attains superpower status from the world. Scher was influenced by her father to draw maps as that was his job when she was a young girl. Many of Scher’s designs have become synonymous with the New York City culture. Scher has collections of her work displayed at New York MoMA, Museum fur Gestaltung Zurich and the library of Congress in Washing DC. Scher is credited with the new identity creation of the Public Theater. She used a promotional graphic system that became highly influential in theatrical promotion for years to come. Scher has work presented at The Philadelphia Museum of Art, The Denver Art Museum, The Victoria and Albert Museum in London, The Bibliotheque nationale de France and in The Centre Georges Pompidou in Paris. In 1996, Scher’s widely imitated identity for the Public Theater won the coveted Beacon Award for integrated corporate design strategy due to its promotional graphic system. In 1998, Scher was named to the Art Directors Club Hall of Fame of Pentagram and later in 2000 she received the Chrysler Award for innovation in Design. Scher has been nominated for four Grammys in the past for Inspiring Designs. She was introduced to the national board of the American Institute of Graphic arts and became the president of the New York chapter from 1998 to 2000. In recent years, 2022, she received the D and AD Special Presidents Award. Scher has been an Alliance Grpahique Internationale member since 1993 and president of this organization from 2009 to 2012 where she retired from the organization's presidency.
